Live pages for “epic” projects

I wrote the markup, foundational CSS and a chuck of the feature-specific JS in both our recently revamped Story pages (June 2010) and the TodayShow cover (March 2011).  Some project highlights:

  • strong emphasis on crisp markup and client-side performance, which achieved rather large performance gains in our rewritten front-end 
    • Today.com’s relaunch was caught by Steve Souders’ HTTP Archive:  http://httparchive.org/viewsite.php?pageid=302643#trends  (I’m proud of those graphs.)
  • Story layout variations based on ad scenarios and page content and story length, triggered by a series of signatures (as classes) on <body>
    • .RunOfSite.adX1 v. .Sponsored.adX2 v. .Sponsored.adX3, etc
  • some rather ridiculously complex JS for our slideshow “slice”, which has a (horrible) matrix of layouts based around image dimensions, viewport and ad intervals—best described in person
  • a vocabulary and styleguide for our IDs and classes between styling, JS-functionality hooks and our own semantics
  • an “OOCSS” effort for the TodayShow cover .block class layer, which I plan to simplify into something readable and send more details about
  • microdata, microformat and RDFa nerdery in our Stories
