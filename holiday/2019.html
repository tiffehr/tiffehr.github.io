2019 Holiday Letter

2019: Five Years for the Price of One?

Work accelerates once again. The New York Times continues to not fail! Shocking, I know. In fact, we’re handling strong reader interest around the impeachment, solid subscription activity and meeting goals around growth…in addition to the usual world-class journalism. News companies are facing some serious headwinds over the next few years but The Times is decently positioned to weather much of it. We’re heading into the toughest year of the four-year “news cycle”; 2020 brings us regular events like Super Bowls, Oscars and extreme weather but also a Summer Olympics and a general election in November.

All my holiday letters include this, but I expect to be exceptionally busy in 2020, working on our document-driven reporting toolset, live politics chats and eventually the election itself.

Family & Travel.  Mom’s health continues to decline but includes some plateaus we can manage. She has grown to like her assisted-living facility as well as her “Tongan Army”. The “army” is an extended family of Tongan-American nurses who cover dayside, with the facility staff taking nightside. The lovely Laloni family — cousins of pro golfer Tony Finau and two NFL players — include the matriarch, her daughter-in-law, two granddaughters and other draftees as needed. In classic form, Dad is helping the army set up their own business, in addition to taking on other Abbington clients. They all adore Mom and even took her to a traditional islander pig roast feast for Thanksgiving as a special guest.

Mom is now reliant on her wheelchair and full assists for standing, which limits where she can go safely. So this year most of my travel was work-related or to be with family. Dad is still on doctor’s orders to reduce his care-taking load, so my non-Utah trips included supervision of him (mom’s orders) at the Mexico timeshare as well as a shoulder-season visit to the Jersey Shore to see old family friends.

My three cats are well. Three! That’s absurd. SMH. Dragon is his usual normal-cat goofy self (see photo, below). Behemoth’s food-related neuroses are getting worse, but the poor lump also has kidney disease so I’ll be shifting his care towards “fospice” (feline hospice), come time. Lioness, my mostly-feral cat, is coming around quickly after we crossed our one-year ownership anniversary in July. I can pick her up; she tolerates nail trims, brushes and baths. Sometimes she accepts full-body pets, too. But still no purring or kneading, so I’ll keep working on her. There’s a normal cat in there somewhere.

Like last year, I spent most of my volunteer hours in the ASPCA’s Kitten Nursery. We saw ~1,200 kittens this year, but most were quickly shuttled to foster homes until they were ready for spay/neuter & adoption. Due to the fostering emphasis, my volunteer duties changed; I became a Nursery Concierge. Sounds fancy, but I was a glorified servant to kittens and/or staff. I handled supplies, cleaning, intake (humans & cats), vet evals and some butt baths (long story). And I also had moments to socialize the kittens, teaching them the perks of humans like warm laps and good pets. It continues to be an ideal antidote to working in news. I plan to do it again next year!

Yorkville is good. My new neighborhood is growing on me. My apartment is a bit drafty, but I have prevention rituals and a drafty winter means a temperate summer. The ASPCA is five minutes away, rather than 60+ minutes, which helps me better balance work, volunteering and other hobbies. I have a nice park nearby, a gym and I may add fencing lessons — in classic NYC form, the ground floor of my building has a bodega, a dry cleaner and a world-class fencing school. Sure, why not?

May 2020 get us all back on track.

Best of 2019, in no particular order

Theater/Music •  I forgot a notable 2018 play: seeing Othello at The Globe theater in London, with Mark Rylance and Andre Holland (!) It was really good; Rylance is a force of nature. •  White Noise at The Public, starring an amazing Daveed Diggs (the original Lafayette/Jefferson in “Hamilton”) •  I finally got to the New York Philharmonic for the debut of composer/conductor Esa-Pekka Salonen’s Gemini work. It was lovely; I have a lot to learn about music in performance  Books •  “La Belle Sauvage” and “The Secret Commonwealth”, the first two of Philip Pullman’s newest trilogy, “The Book of Dust”, which extends the mid-90’s trilogy, “His Dark Materials” (relatedly, HBO’s new adaptation of the latter is decent; the new books are stupendous) •  “Good Omens”, by Neil Gaiman & Terry Pratchett (I very belatedly caught up with this 1990 novel, to get ahead of Amazon’s delightful adaptation starring Michael Sheen and David Tennant; tied, books vs. series) •  “Say Nothing: A True Story of Murder and Memory in Northern Ireland”, by Patrick Radden Keefe, building on an interest in the Troubles after seeing “The Ferryman” (a play) last year  Media •  Chernobyl, HBO’s riveting series, paired with an excellent behind-the-adaptation podcast hosted by Peter Sagal •  Bundyville, a podcast about the anti-government conspiracy theories driving domestic terrorism in the West •  Leaving Neverland, Surviving R. Kelly, At the Heart of Gold, each a searing documentary series, bringing belated reckoning for sexual abuse survivors of Michael Jackson, R. Kelly and Larry Nassar (respectively) •  When They See Us, a Netflix dramatization of the Central Park Five story with unforgettable performances

Did I watch normal, unserious stuff? Sure, lots! But it wasn’t particularly notable.
