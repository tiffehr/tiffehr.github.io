2014 Holiday Letter

2014:  Not my best balancing of work/life but A+ comp time!

Work was a lot.  The volume of major news events scheduled on even-numbered years makes for some time-intensive, high-profile combinations.  2014 saw me working the Sochi Winter Olympics, all six weeks of the men’s World Cup and the U.S. midterm elections.  Each are big efforts on their own; combined…well, I banked a lot of comp time.

Between the big events, I helped on the launches of two new NYT sub-brands: The Upshot, data-oriented slice of economics, politics and sports; and First Draft, a newsletter/daily live-blog for U.S. politics.  And in case that sounds like room to breathe, I also spent most of late May living in a hotel in the French Quarter of New Orleans, training j-school students in an intensive, two-week newspaper bootcamp run by The Times.  

May 2015 be as challenging—but more sustainable.

Moulson is now a cancer survivor.  My repeated gags about Moulson’s old age took its revenge this year.  Mo was diagnosed with lymphoma, via one minor tumor.  Two surgeries removed it, yet lymphoma is the kind of cancer that will return.  Mo took advantage of his recovery time to amplify his ‘old man’ habits, of course.  He’s now pretty much a cat, sleeping on the couch by our warmest winter radiator.  Rest assured we have some quality time to spend together yet.

(Above:  Moulson defecting families last Christmas, to join Lisa, John & Milo.)

My family expanded their horizons. My brother Adam opened a second bagel shop, taking another step toward his bagel empire.  My parents toured Croatia, Montenegro, Bosnia, Slovenia, Mexico, Nicaragua, El Salvador, Honduras, Guatemala and Belize.  In their own holiday letter, Mom finally shared some details about her health.  She’s dealing with a Parkinsonian dementia—a rare version called corticobasal degeneration.  Mom has a phalanx of therapists helping her adjust to diminished abilities on a few fronts.  My dad’s patient care makes a huge difference as well.  Mom enjoys the stimulation and activity of traveling, so 2015 already includes another international tour or two for them.

My own vacation time was diverse.  I attended two notable weddings this year:  a whirlwind trip to Cape Town, South Africa and a too-short return to Seattle.  Cape Town was fantastic.  I spent my birthday on the top of Table Mountain, waving at giant container ships rounding the Cape of Good Hope.  My brief return to Seattle was complex.  Mo* and I flew to Utah, then I drove up to Seattle.  Mo stayed with my parents, providing some healing time since they lost their dog earlier this year to cancer (damned cancer).  Both Seattle and the wedding were fantastic.  I dearly miss it and my friends there—thankfully all are doing just fine.

Lastly, I spent part of November living as a retiree with my parents in Mexico’s timeshare racket.  My parents also timeshared a grandchild, in a way.  Long story short:  three years ago my good friends Lisa & John joined us in Mexico.  At the time Lisa was seven months pregnant.  This year they joined us again, along with their son, Milo.  Mom and Dad were delighted.  We learned it takes five adults to keep up with one toddler.  After Milo & family returned home, my parents and I moped around the resort, missing Milo’s energy and pool-choice decisiveness.
