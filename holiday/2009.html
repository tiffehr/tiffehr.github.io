2009 Holiday Letter

Happy 2010: The Future! It’s a relief to say that. I’m glad 2009 is over. It was a year of downgraded expectations and holding fast—little forward progress worth noting. So good riddence to the ’Aughts and on to the ‘Terrible Teens’ (let’s hope that name doesn’t stick).

Busy. This year’s letter is shorter than previous years largely because I’ve been immersed in long-term projects both at work and around my house. The kind that are boring to talk about. At msnbc.com, we’re still hard at work on our story page redesign. The project has taken more than a year and led me into technologies I never intended to learn. It’s been a challenge in many ways—both good and bad. I’d share more details, however we’re in the thick of it as I write this retrospective. More reflection on the news industry and company financials in my 2011 letter, to be sure.

Regarding my house, I had for sale for a year but found no serious buyers. I got a lot of first-time buyers looking for rentable space/income but they bought dime-a-dozen townhomes instead. I’ll try it again next year if Seattle’s market comes around. The analysts I read seem to think Seattle is 10+ months behind other cities’ housing improvements (should you think any of them areimproving). Hopefully next year’s letter will include significant progress on that front. I’m ready to move to a more efficient urban footprint, though I do have a new housing-selection requirement….

A Dog! After five years talk, I adopted a dog! He’s a rescue dog—about 6 years old—named Moulson. He’s had that name for at least a year or more so I’m not at liberty to change it. Yes, I sound like a dumbass at the dog park, yelling for a dog who’s name sounds like a crappy beer. But he is a great dog and other dog-park citizens envy his demeanor. He’s had an unknown abusive past but is adjusting with remarkable swiftness to being a contented urban pet. Also: he’s adorable (see photo). Expect my future New Year letters to include lots of dog reporting.

Family. There is not much new to report on the family front. Mom and Dad are ensconced in their ski lodge home, volunteering like crazy while maintaining a mild-mannered retiree front. My brother Adam’s bagel shop in Park City is doing well, though sadly he lost two employees (and friends) to a car collision this summer. The shop regulars made some amazing gestures of support.  Both parents and brother are making the most of Park City, Utah.
